# SSCC-p00005: Choice Management

**Level**: prd | **Status**: Active | **Implements**: -

**Purpose:** The engine presents players with explicit, legal options at
decision points during gameplay. Choices follow a defined lifecycle from
being offered through resolution, expiry, or cancellation. The engine
tracks active choices, pauses timeline advancement while unresolved
choices exist, and provides explanations when choices are blocked.
The full choice vocabulary is determinable at pack load time, enabling
upfront validation of all choice identifiers and action references.

## Assertions

A. The engine SHALL offer each **choice** as a named, typed option associated with a specific player.

B. When a pack is loaded, the engine SHALL validate every declared choice identifier and action reference against the loaded rule set.

C. The engine SHALL track each choice instance through exactly one of these lifecycle states: offered, selected, resolved, expired, or cancelled.

D. When a choice transitions from one lifecycle state to another, the engine SHALL emit a system event identifying the choice, the player, and the new state.

E. While one or more offered choices remain unresolved, the engine SHALL pause timeline advancement until each such choice is either selected, expired, or cancelled.

F. When a player selects a choice, the engine SHALL validate that the choice is currently in the offered state for that player before accepting the selection.

G. When a proposed choice is blocked, the engine SHALL return the set of reason keys explaining why the choice is not available.

H. The engine SHALL enumerate all currently legal choices for a given player and game state on request.

I. When a choice's associated expiry condition is met, the engine SHALL transition that choice to the expired state.

J. When a choice has an associated window and that window closes, the engine SHALL expire that choice.
